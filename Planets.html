Wandering Worlds of the Cosmos


Mercury

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun, with no atmosphere and wild temperature shifts.

Venus

Hot and hostile, Venus has thick clouds of CO₂ and sulfuric acid with crushing pressure.

Earth

Our home — the only known planet to support life, with oceans, continents, and atmosphere.


Mars

The Red Planet, home to dusty landscapes, polar ice caps, and robotic exploration missions.

Jupiter

The gas giant king with swirling storms, rings, and over 90 moons — including Ganymede.

Saturn

Famous for its rings, Saturn is a beautiful gas giant with moons like Titan and Enceladus.

Uranus

A pale blue ice giant that spins on its side, giving it bizarre seasons and a faint ring system.

Neptune

The windiest planet in the solar system, deep blue with a dark storm system and icy clouds.

Pluto

A dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, once the ninth planet. Known for its icy heart-shaped plain.
