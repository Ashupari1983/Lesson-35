Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was NASA’s historic 1969 mission that landed the first humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked the lunar surface while Michael Collins remained in orbit. It marked a major achievement in human space exploration.

Voyager 1

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 explored Jupiter and Saturn before continuing into interstellar space. It is now the farthest human-made object from Earth, still transmitting data after more than 45 years.

Curiosity Rover

Curiosity landed on Mars in 2012 to study the planet’s surface, climate, and potential habitability. It has been exploring Gale Crater and continues to make significant scientific discoveries.

Rosetta

ESA’s Rosetta mission was launched in 2004 and became the first spacecraft to orbit and land on a comet — 67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko. It provided critical insights into the early solar system.

Chandrayaan-3

In 2023, ISRO’s Chandrayaan-3 successfully landed near the Moon’s south pole. It marked India’s first successful soft landing and expanded global lunar exploration capabilities.

Tianwen-1

Tianwen-1, launched by CNSA in 2020, included an orbiter, lander, and rover — Zhurong. It made China the second country to operate a rover on Mars.

James Webb Space Telescope

Launched in 2021, JWST is the most advanced space telescope to date. It observes the universe in infrared, revealing early galaxies, exoplanets, and cosmic phenomena.

Sputnik 1

Sputnik 1, launched by the Soviet Union in 1957, was the first artificial satellite. It began the space age and triggered the space race between the US and USSR.
